Review System

The 4-cadence
review architecture

Different aspects of your wealth system require different review frequencies. This four-cadence architecture ensures everything is maintained at the right interval — nothing overlooked, nothing over-analyzed.

⏰
The Non-Negotiable Rule of Reviews
Reviews must be scheduled in advance and treated as fixed appointments — not flexible intentions. Block them in your calendar now: one 15-minute slot per week, one 45-minute slot per month, one 3-hour slot per quarter, and one full-day slot per year. These are the most valuable financial hours you will spend.
Weekly — 15 Minutes
Portfolio Pulse Check
  • Check for stop-loss triggers on all positions
  • Review portfolio allocation — any >5% drift?
  • Confirm automated investments executed
  • Note any significant news affecting held positions
  • Write 1 sentence in investment journal
Every Friday, 15 min
Monthly — 45 Minutes
Financial Health Review
  • Update Monthly Investment Log template
  • Review expenses vs. budget categories
  • Calculate savings rate for the month
  • Check goal progress on goals tracker
  • Review any positions requiring thesis update
  • Update wealth dashboard KPIs
1st of month, 45 min
Quarterly — 3 Hours
Strategic Portfolio Review
  • Complete Quarterly Review template
  • Full portfolio rebalancing assessment
  • Review all thesis positions — update status

  • Income growth initiative progress
  • Re-read your investment plan (PB01)

The Final 8

The 8 operating principles
of the Capital Code system

Synthesized from all five playbooks — these are the eight principles that govern the entire Capital Code architecture. Pin them. Review them quarterly. Let them override your emotions when the system is tested.

01
📐
The System Executes. You Supervise.
Your automated investments run whether you feel confident or fearful, whether markets are up or down. Your role is to build the system correctly and supervise it at defined intervals — not to intervene continuously. The best financial decision is often no decision at all.
02
📈
Compound Time, Not Just Capital
Every month you delay starting costs more than any investment return can recover. A 25-year-old investing €500/month at 8% reaches €1.9M by 65. A 35-year-old with the same contribution reaches €745K. The 10-year delay costs €1.15M. Time is the resource that cannot be bought back.
03
🔍
Review the System, Not the Market
Your quarterly and annual reviews should focus on your system's adherence to plan — not on market commentary, predictions, or macro narratives. Ask: "Is my allocation correct? Is my savings rate on target? Is my thesis intact?" Not: "What will the market do next quarter?"
04
🌊
Crises are Features, Not Bugs
Every major wealth-building opportunity in history arrived disguised as a catastrophe. 2009, 2020, 2022 — all produced extraordinary entry points for the patient, liquid investor. Your crisis protocol from Playbook 02 exists precisely to transform your emotional response to fear into a systematic, pre-programmed wealth-building action.
05
💎
Quality Over Complexity
A simple portfolio of 3–5 high-quality, low-cost ETFs, systematically invested, consistently rebalanced, and never panic-sold will outperform 80% of actively managed strategies over 20 years. Complexity is not sophistication — it is usually a source of additional costs, additional friction, and additional opportunities for emotional error.
06
📏
Measure What Matters
Track your savings rate, your net worth trajectory, your savings-to-goal progress, and your system adherence. Do not obsess over daily portfolio value, individual position performance, or short-term returns. The metrics you track determine the behaviors you reinforce — choose them with deliberate care.
07
🔄
Iterate the System Annually
The wealth system you build today is version 1.0. Your January annual review is the annual upgrade. Adjust your risk profile as life changes. Update your FI number as expenses change. Refine your tax strategies as income grows. The system should evolve with you — not remain a static document gathering digital dust.
08
🌱
Wealth is a Practice, Not an Event
There is no single decision, trade, or investment that creates wealth. Wealth is built through thousands of small, consistent, correct decisions made over decades. The discipline of the weekly journal entry, the monthly review, the quarterly rebalance, and the annual audit — these mundane habits, compounded over 20 years, produce extraordinary outcomes.
